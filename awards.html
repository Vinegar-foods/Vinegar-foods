STAR MICHELIN

Compared to the Oscar of cinema or the Grammy of music, the Michelin star is an important "award" in the world culinary village. Michelin star is essentially a symbol to evaluate the quality of a restaurant. Every year, Michelin-starred restaurants are honored in the world's leading culinary guide - The Michelin Guide - dating from 1900. Restaurants that own this flower-shaped star symbol will have a reputation as "a kite in the wind". It takes many diners months to get a chance to enjoy food in Michelin-starred restaurants

Beefsteak a dish consisting of flat slices of beef, usually grilled, pan-fried, or broiling over high heat. Tender cuts of meat are cut from the loin and ribs are cooked quickly, using dry heat, and served whole. Less tender cuts of beef from beef shoulder blades and rump processed with moist heat or mechanically tenderized. This is the dish that made the restaurant's name, with all kinds.
